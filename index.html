Interactive Generation of Laparoscopic Videos with Diffusion Models

Authors anonymized for blind review
MICCAI 2024
Code

Abstract

Generative AI in general, and synthetic visual data generation in specific, hold much promise for benefiting surgical training. Current training methods, however, primarily rely on reading materials and observing live surgeries, which can be time-consuming and impractical. In this work, we developed an approach, that works towards improving the training process. We use diffusion models in combination with a zero-shot video diffusion method that allows us to interactively generate realistic laparoscopic images and videos by specifying a surgical action through text and guiding the generation with tool positions through segmentation masks. We conduct our experiments on the publicly available Cholec dataset family and evaluate fidelity and factual correctness using a surgical action recognition model as well as the pixel-wise F1-score for the spatial control of tool generation. As a result, our video generation approach achieves an FID of 38.097 and an F1-score of 0.71.
